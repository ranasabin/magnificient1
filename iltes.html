IELTS – International English Language Testing System

IELTS measures a candidate’s ability to use all four language skills — listening, reading, writing, and speaking in English. It is jointly managed by the University of Cambridge ESOL Examinations, British Council, and IDP Education Australia. The test is designed for those who plan to study or work in an English-speaking country and uses international varieties of English including British, American, Australian, and South African.

Around 6,000 academic and professional institutions globally recognize IELTS scores as a valid indicator of English proficiency. Over 1 million aspirants take IELTS annually to pursue career goals abroad. It is offered across 120 countries and is one of the fastest-growing English language tests in the world.

IELTS is widely accepted in the UK, Australia, Canada, Ireland, and New Zealand for higher education and is gaining ground in the US as an alternative to TOEFL, although not all US institutions accept it.
