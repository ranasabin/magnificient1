SAT – Scholastic Assessment Test

Students applying for undergraduate programs in the USA may be required to take the SAT in addition to the TOEFL. While some colleges require it, others may only recommend it. For competitive college admissions and scholarships, taking the SAT is highly recommended.

There are two types of SAT: SAT I (Reasoning Test) and SAT II (Subject Tests). SAT I covers critical reading, math, and writing, while SAT II focuses on specific subjects like Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and more.

SAT exams are paper-based and typically offered around six times a year in Nepal at centers such as Lincoln School, St. Xavier’s School, or Rato Bangala School. On any exam date, you can choose to take either SAT I or up to three SAT II subjects.

Test Overview

  • Total Score: 1600 (800 for Math and 800 for Reading, Writing & Language)
  • Test Dates: Check the testing calendar at www.collegeboard.com
  • Cost: USD 113.50 (With Writing); USD 98.50 (Without Writing)
  • Register: By mail using a dollar-denominated bank draft, or online with an international credit card. See website for details.
  • Website: www.collegeboard.com
